**Parameter**
- A **parameter** is a quantity that characterizes a system but is **not directly measurable**.
- It usually represents an intrinsic property, such as mass, charge, or coupling strength.
- In **estimation theory**, it’s the **unknown value** you are trying to **infer** or **estimate**.
- Example: In quantum mechanics, a rotation angle $\theta$ applied via a unitary is a parameter.

**Observable**
- An **observable** is a **measurable quantity**, directly measurable.
- In quantum mechanics, it corresponds to a **Hermitian operator**; its eigenvalues are possible outcomes of a measurement.
- In estimation theory, observables give you **data** that help estimate the underlying parameter.
- Example: Measuring $\sigma_z$ (Pauli Z) on a qubit gives an observable outcome (+1 or -1).

Simple Analogy
- **Parameter**: The **hidden setting** of a knob (e.g., the angle you rotate a qubit).
- **Observable**: The **readout** on a screen that you use to guess what that setting was.

In non-relativistic QM e.g. mass is parameter, observable.
